Mactan Shrine

‹ ›

The Mactan Shrine serves as a site of one of the defining moments in Philippine history - the Battle of Mactan. Located in Punta Engaño, Lapu-Lapu City, Mactan Shrine is a must-see for everyone interested in history.

In 1519, General Ferdinand Magellan, a Portuguese navigator, headed a Spanish fleet in the name of Spain during King Charles I to find a western sea route to Asia and get some spices. They landed in Cebu Island in 1521, where they started Christianity and persuaded some chiefs to pledge their allegiance to the King of Spain.

The Mactan Shrine commemorates the battle between the Spanish and the Visayan native people on April 27, 1521. It was erected on the supposed spot where the battle between Chief or Datu of Mactan Lapu-Lapu and Portuguese explorer General Ferdinand Magellan took place. The historical place was then preserved and houses a shrine dedicated in honor of Lapu-Lapu, Ferdinand Magellan, and the Battle of Mactan.

Guisi Lighthouse

‹ ›

The Guisi Lighthouse, located in Nueva Valencia, Guimaras, is one of the oldest lighthouses in the Philippines, constructed in 1896. This charming structure, with its rustic white facade, is not only an active navigational aid but also a captivating glimpse into the rich maritime history of the area. Visitors are drawn to the lighthouse not just for its historical value but also for the stunning panoramic views it offers. Climbing the steps to the top rewards you with breathtaking vistas of the azure waters and the lush greenery surrounding the lighthouse.

Surrounding the lighthouse are picturesque landscapes, making it a perfect spot for photography, picnics, or simply soaking in the natural beauty. The gentle sound of waves crashing against the rocky shore creates a peaceful ambiance, inviting tourists to linger a little longer. The site is accessible daily from 8 AM to 8 PM, allowing ample opportunity to explore and enjoy the area throughout the day. As you stroll around, take the time to appreciate the local flora and fauna, which add to the area’s charm.

Fort San Pedro

‹ ›

Fort San Pedro is situated at A. Pigafetta Street, Cebu City, is the oldest and smallest fort in the Philippines and is one of Cebu’s pride. Lying in Plaza Independencia in the pier area of Cebu, the fort holds a rich history of the island and the historical events during the Spanish rule.

Fort San Pedro or Fuerza de San Pedro is a triangular bastión fort. Its two sides are fronting the sea, and the other edge is facing the land where the entrance to the fort is located. You can see cannons installed on the sides facing the sea, while a sturdy fence made of wood is placed in front.

At present, Fort San Pedro is a historical park under the care and administration of the Cebu City government. You can visit the museum inside the fort that holds several preserved Spanish artifacts, such as paintings, sculptures, old photographs and items, and documents. The spacious lawn of the fort now serves as a perfect venue for occasions like weddings, birthdays, and others, but only with a permit from the designated personnel.
